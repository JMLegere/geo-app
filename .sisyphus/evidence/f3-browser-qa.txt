### Scenario 2: Concealed vs Unexplored Opacity
- Status: ✅ PASS
- Concealed opacity (effective fog density): 0.95
  - Measured pixel brightness: 36 (RGB: 33,33,42)
  - Cells immediately adjacent to player's observed cell
- Unexplored opacity (effective fog density): 1.0
  - Measured pixel brightness: 25 (RGB: 22,22,32)
  - Cells further from player
- Concealed IS lighter than unexplored (36 > 25) ✅
- Methodology:
  - Horizontal brightness scan at y=490 through player cell:
      x=200-360: brightness=25 (unexplored/undetected)
      x=380-420: brightness=36 (concealed — adjacent to player cell)
      x=440-520: brightness=230+ (observed — player's cell)
      x=540-640: brightness=36 (concealed — adjacent to player cell)
      x=660+: brightness=25 (unexplored/undetected)
  - Architecture confirmed from source code (fog_state.dart):
      FogState.concealed = density 0.95 (hole in fog-base, mid-fog at 0.95)
      FogState.unexplored = density 1.0 (fully opaque base fog)
  - Visual inspection (look_at on s2-crop-player-cell.png):
      "Adjacent cells: Light gray/off-white, slightly darker than central cell"
      "Farther cells: Dark navy/charcoal, significantly darker"
